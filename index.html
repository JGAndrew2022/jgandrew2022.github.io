About

I am a senior at the University of Colorado Boulder pursuing dual B.S. degrees in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science. I research optimal control and analysis of PDE-governed systems under Dr. Emily Jensen’s in Electrical Engineering at CU. I also spent this past summer as an undergrad research fellow in the Patterns and PDEs REU at UC Irvine, where I studied pattern formation in reaction-diffusion models of biological growth. I also worked for two years as a system administrator on the Linux Platform/DevOps team for CU Boulder's Office of Information Technology, where I gained experience writing softwaere for system administration, automation, and monitoring.

Currently, I am applying to PhD programs in engineering and applied mathematics, with an interest in optimal control and design of dynamical systems, safety and verification of autonomous systems, and analysis and numerical methods for PDE-governed models. I am proficient in programming with MATLAB, Python, and C++, and I have hands-on experience with Linux, git, Jupyter, and Agile methodologies. I also have research experience in partial differential equations, optimal control, numerical PDEs, and asymptotic methods.
