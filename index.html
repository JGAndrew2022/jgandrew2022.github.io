I am an undergraduate at the University of Colorado in my junior year pursuing two Bachelors of Science in applied math and computer science. I am most interested in the intersections of these two disciplines, such as in computing, modeling, optimization, and control theory.

Currently, I work as an undergraduate research fellow under Dr. Emily Jensen of the University of Colorado’s Department of Electrical Engineering to study the modeling and analysis of wave-like systems with non-linear dynamics, such as soft-bodied crawling robots and electrical circuits. My ongoing project involves deriving the optimal dynamics of a soft-bodied crawling robot to inform the energy-efficient design of such systems. In addition, I work as a Linux assistant system administrator at the CU Office of Information Technology to manage the university’s server system.

I am proficient in programming with MATLAB, Python, and C++, and I have hands-on experience with Linux, git, Jupyter, and Agile methodologies. My academic and research background has also equipped me with strong skills in partial differential equations, nonlinear dynamics, linear algebra, and numerical methods.

My relevant coursework completed at CU includes Data Structures, Algorithms, Programming Languages, Machine Learning, Discrete Math, Calculus III, Differential Equations, Linear Algebra, Applied Probability, Real Analysis, Complex Analysis, Markov Processes, Partial Differential Equations, Theory of Computation, and Cybersecurity.
