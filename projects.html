Optimal Actuation of Peristaltic Crawlers

This presentation was part of my summer research under Dr. Emily Jensen through the CU Boulder Summer Program for Undergraduate Research. The project focuses on the design and analysis of peristaltic crawlers—soft-bodied robots whose motion mimics that of an earthworm. These robots are of significant interest in robotics due to their potential applications in minimally invasive surgery and infrastructure maintenance.

My work involves the analytical study of the non-linear dynamics of a peristaltic crawler under conditions where the nonlinearity can be treated as a perturbation. The primary objective is to identify the parameters that most influence the energy efficiency of these robots, which is important for their development given their limited power supplies and inefficient locomotion mode. This research is ongoing under Dr. Jensen through the 2024–2025 academic year, with plans to submit a paper detailing our work to the 64th IEEE Conference on Decision and Control this spring.

Modeling Momentum in Tennis Matches

This paper was written for problem C of the 2024 Mathematical Contest in Modeling, hosted by the Consortium for Mathematics and its Applications. In the paper, my team and I create a mathematical model for momentum in tennis. We then use this model to prove momentum is important in winning a tennis match, and identify key variables that can induce momentum episodes.
